Research Overview

This research is crucial in addressing some of the biggest challenges in modern agriculture: water wastage, cost of production, and cost to consumers. As global resources become increasingly strained, efficient water management is essential for sustainability. Our goal was to develop a system that collects data from indoor plants and outdoor gardens, with the potential to scale to small farms. By leveraging data science and machine learning, we aimed to automate plant watering in a way that minimizes waste, optimizes costs for farmers, and ensures affordability for consumers.

Database

For our research, we use MySQL to store and manage the data collected from various sensors. MySQL is a relational database management system that allows us to organize data into related tables for efficient access and retrieval.

How It Works

The diagram above shows how different tables in our database are linked together. For instance:

  • Sensor Table: Contains information about the sensors, such as their ID, location, type, and whether they are indoors or outdoors.
  • Soil Moisture and Temperature Tables: Store readings from soil moisture and temperature sensors, with references to the specific sensor IDs and timestamps.
  • Weather Data Tables: Include information like wind speed, humidity, solar radiation, and rainfall, each linked to their corresponding sensors and timestamps.

The relational structure ensures that data is consistent and avoids duplication. For example, instead of storing sensor details repeatedly for every reading, we use references (foreign keys) to link each reading back to the sensor table.

Why MySQL?

MySQL offers several advantages over spreadsheets:

  • Scalability: Databases can handle large volumes of data efficiently, unlike spreadsheets which may become slow and unwieldy as data grows.
  • Relationships: MySQL’s relational model lets you link tables, making it easier to manage complex datasets and maintain data integrity.
  • Query Capabilities: SQL allows for powerful queries to analyze data, filter results, and perform calculations directly within the database.
  • Multi-User Access: Databases support simultaneous access by multiple users, making collaboration easier.
  • Security: MySQL provides robust security features, ensuring data protection and controlled access.

In simple terms, using a database like MySQL allows us to store, access, and analyze data efficiently and accurately, which is crucial for our machine learning models.

Understanding the Data

The dashboards above provide insights into the data collected from various sensors within a specific time range. Here's what the graphs show:

  • Wind: The wind graph displays wind speed, gusts, and direction over time. Wind plays a significant role in soil moisture evaporation, and monitoring it helps us predict water requirements for the soil.
  • Soil Moisture: The soil moisture chart tracks the moisture levels in different parts of the garden. This helps in identifying dry spots that require irrigation.
  • Solar Radiation: Displays the intensity of solar radiation and UV exposure. High radiation levels directly impact the rate of soil drying and plant water uptake.
  • Outdoor Temperature: Shows fluctuations in temperature. Higher temperatures increase evaporation, while lower temperatures slow it down.

By analyzing these variables together, we can better understand the environmental factors influencing soil health and optimize water usage.
